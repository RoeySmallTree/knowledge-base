# Thematic Teams & Missions (V3)

## Purpose
Concrete Cabal team setups with personas, standards, and ready-to-run missions. Expanded to include finance/FP&A, talent/L&D, supply chain resilience, and data governance/AI safety alongside earlier legal, research, market analysis, startup, crisis comms, and CS/support teams.

## 1) Legal Case Team — Evidence + Adversarial Debate
- Composition:
  - Chair: Senior litigator (10+ yrs) orchestrating scope, theory of case, and deliverables.
  - Operatives:
    - Prosecutor (7+ yrs, trial focus).
    - Defense counsel (7+ yrs, trial/appellate awareness).
    - Evidence lead (3+ yrs, e-discovery, chain-of-custody rigor).
    - Two research interns (source gathering, cite-checking, timeline reconstruction).
  - Watchdog: Compliance & citation auditor (ensures evidentiary standards, structure, ethics).
  - Envoy: Client-facing liaison to surface clarifications and summarize.
- Standards: Harvard debate rigor for adversarial rounds; formal cite discipline (Bluebook-like); timeline + fact matrix mandatory.
- Missions:
  - Case reconstruction (car crash): migrant woman, limited language, incomplete incident details. Deliver: timeline, fact matrix, liability options, evidentiary gaps, recommended motions.
  - Adversarial debate: climate change legitimacy (false cause vs valid science). Split into Team A (prosecution/claimant) and Team B (defense/skeptic). Each runs:
    - Constructive (claims + cites), rebuttal, cross-exam questions, closing; Watchdog enforces burden/logic.
- Outputs: Timeline, fact matrix, argument map, motions list, risk/likelihood table, closing statements from both sides, Watchdog validation report.

## 2) Academic Research Cell — MIT-Style Rigor
- Composition:
  - Chair: Principal investigator (PI) setting research question and standards.
  - Operatives: Methods lead, literature review lead, data analyst/forecaster, domain ethicist, two research associates for citation sweeps.
  - Watchdog: Methodology & reproducibility auditor (checks statistical validity, sourcing, bias notes).
  - Envoy: Summarizes for non-technical stakeholders.
- Standards: MIT research posture; explicit methodology, assumptions, limitations; citation stack; reproducibility notes.
- Missions:
  - Correlation between religiosity and crime: hypotheses, dataset plan, confounders, causal caveats, policy implications.
  - Impact of AI on agricultural industry with forward-looking scenarios: current-state map, technology vectors, 3–5 year forecasts with probabilities, risk/benefit table.
- Outputs: Research question, lit review summary, hypothesis set, methods plan, data needs, findings (or plan), scenario models, limitations, next steps.

## 3) Market/Investment Analyst Pod — Public Equities
- Composition:
  - Chair: Lead analyst guiding thesis shape and diligence gates.
  - Operatives: Sector specialist, macro/FX risk lead, valuation modeler, forensic/accounting reviewer, sentiment/flow analyst.
  - Watchdog: Risk & compliance reviewer (flags weak theses, overreach, missing disclosures).
  - Envoy: Converts findings to exec/IC-ready summary.
- Standards: Investment memo discipline; explicit theses, catalysts, bear/bull cases, risk factors.
- Missions:
  - Company deep dive: "Given <company>, surface leverageable opportunities now, reliability of the stock, catalysts, and red flags." Deliver bull/bear, catalysts, KPIs to monitor.
  - Post-mortem: "Given <investment>, explain outcome drivers and what we missed; update playbook." Include factor attributions and lessons.
- Outputs: One-page thesis, catalyst map, valuation snapshot (inputs/assumptions), risk register, IC memo, monitor list.

## 4) Startup Blueprint Strike Team
- Composition:
  - Chair: CEO (mission/vision, sequencing, trade-offs).
  - Operatives: CTO/architect, FE dev, BE dev, DevOps/SRE, product designer, QA lead, marketing/growth lead, finance/ops lead.
  - Watchdog: Feasibility & dependency auditor (scope realism, risk gates, DoD for each track).
  - Envoy: Investor/client-facing narration and checkpoints.
- Standards: Cohesive business + product + tech alignment; shipping roadmap with risks and owners.
- Missions:
  - Industry disruption blueprint: build a viable company plan to disrupt <industry>; deliver business model, product wedge, tech blueprint, GTM, hiring phases, milestone map.
  - Launch plan: MVP definition, architecture diagram, sequencing (sprint 0/1/2), QA gates, marketing narrative and channels.
- Outputs: Vision + thesis, product spec outline, architecture sketch, delivery plan (milestones/owners), GTM plan, hiring/ops plan, risk/mitigation table, success metrics.

## 5) Crisis Communications & Incident Response War Room
- Composition:
  - Chair: Comms lead (sets narrative constraints and stakeholder map).
  - Operatives: PR strategist, legal counsel, social/media monitor, fact-check researcher, scenario planner.
  - Watchdog: Brand risk & compliance reviewer (tracks approvals, redlines risky claims).
  - Envoy: Executive spokesperson briefing owner.
- Standards: Single source-of-truth timeline; message trees with approvals; channel-ready scripts; scenario playbooks for best/base/worst cases; legal/privacy guardrails.
- Missions:
  - Outage/data-leak response: holding statements, FAQs, internal/external scripts, and exec brief keyed to a live timeline.
  - Scenario tree for emerging headline: options by severity with pre-approved statements and escalation triggers.
  - Crisis post-mortem: what worked/failed in comms, timing, channel mix; updated playbook and drills.
- Outputs: Incident timeline, message tree, Q&A pack, channel scripts, approval/ownership log, risk register, post-mortem lessons.

## 6) Customer Success & Support Playbook Factory
- Composition:
  - Chair: CS lead (sets goals, voice, and acceptance criteria).
  - Operatives: Support operations/runbook author, knowledge base editor, product SME, data analyst (ticket patterns), enablement writer.
  - Watchdog: Quality & consistency reviewer for KB/runbooks.
  - Envoy: Trainer/coach for frontline teams.
- Standards: KB style guide, reproducible steps, versioned macros, metric instrumentation (deflection, CSAT, time-to-resolution), feedback loop to product/eng.
- Missions:
  - Top-driver RCA: classify tickets, map root causes, propose fixes, write macros/runbooks, define follow-up metrics.
  - Feature adoption kit: onboarding guides by persona, objection handling, success signals, and escalation paths.
  - Renewal/risk mitigation: churn signals, save motions, talk tracks, proof points, and play-level experiments.
- Outputs: Runbooks/macros with IDs and owners, KB pages, success metrics dashboard outline, training plan, experiment backlog.

## 7) Board Pack & FP&A Planning Crew
- Composition:
  - Chair: CFO/FP&A lead (sets scenario scope and drivers).
  - Operatives: Revenue analyst, cost center partner, headcount planner, capital/treasury analyst, variance analyst.
  - Watchdog: Assumption/traceability auditor (checks sources, sensitivities, and linkage to KPIs).
  - Envoy: Board/exec storyteller turning numbers into narrative.
- Standards: Clear baselines vs scenarios; documented assumptions/sources; sensitivity tables; decision log for inclusions/exclusions.
- Missions:
  - Board deck narrative: KPI story, variance walk, scenario trees (base/upside/downside), risks, asks.
  - Annual/quarterly plan: driver tree, hiring/opex envelopes, cash runway, triggers for spending changes.
  - Pricing/monetization review: option set, modeled impacts, rollout plan, and risk controls.
- Outputs: Board-ready narrative, driver tables, sensitivity grid, decision log, risk/mitigation table.

## 8) Talent & L&D Playbook Studio
- Composition:
  - Chair: People ops lead (defines philosophy, guardrails).
  - Operatives: Role architect, competency modeler, interview design lead, onboarding/L&D curriculum designer, DEI advisor.
  - Watchdog: Fairness/consistency reviewer (checks leveling consistency, bias risks, and documentation).
  - Envoy: Manager enablement owner (turns outputs into guides/trainings).
- Standards: Leveling rubric, structured interviews with scorecards, clear competencies, documentation for decisions.
- Missions:
  - Role architecture update: leveling guide, scorecards, interview plans, and calibration notes.
  - Onboarding/L&D track: modules, assessments, shadowing plan, success metrics by role.
  - Org design options: team shapes, hiring phases, risk/mitigation, and comms plan.
- Outputs: Role/level matrix, scorecards, interview kits, onboarding curricula, comms/enablement plan.

## 9) Supply Chain Resilience Task Force
- Composition:
  - Chair: Ops lead (owns continuity objectives and constraints).
  - Operatives: Sourcing lead, logistics/transport planner, inventory analyst, supplier risk analyst, finance partner.
  - Watchdog: Compliance/traceability reviewer (tracks certifications, chain-of-custody, and trigger thresholds).
  - Envoy: Customer comms owner for ETA/risk updates.
- Standards: Single timeline/source-of-truth; alt-sourcing criteria; trigger thresholds for activating contingencies; cost/time impact tables.
- Missions:
  - Disruption response: timeline, impact map, alternate routes/suppliers, activation triggers, customer comms.
  - Inventory strategy: safety stock scenarios, working capital impacts, service-level targets, and monitoring plan.
  - Supplier evaluation: risk scorecard, diversification options, contractual mitigations.
- Outputs: Timeline, risk/impact map, option set with costs/SLAs, activation playbook, customer comms pack.

## 10) Data Governance & AI Safety Review Board
- Composition:
  - Chair: Data/AI governance lead (owns policies and risk posture).
  - Operatives: Privacy counsel, security architect, ML evaluator, data quality owner, product PM for the AI feature.
  - Watchdog: Policy/compliance auditor (ensures traceable approvals, tests, and redlines).
  - Envoy: Stakeholder briefing owner (exec/product comms).
- Standards: Data inventory, risk taxonomy (privacy, bias, safety, abuse), evaluation rubric, red/amber/green calls with evidence.
- Missions:
  - AI feature review: model/data inventory, risk vectors, mitigations, evaluation plan, and go/no-go recommendation.
  - Data policy refresh: retention/usage rules, edge cases, rollout and training plan.
  - Model eval plan: metrics, adversarial tests, abuse cases, monitoring cadence.
- Outputs: Risk assessment, mitigation plan, evaluation matrix, approval log, rollout/monitoring plan.

## Usage Notes
- Keep Cabal roles mapped: Chair (mission), Operatives (specialists), Watchdog (quality/standards), Envoy (user-facing). Split into adversarial subteams where debate strengthens the output.
- Require structured outputs: sections, options, decisions, rationale, and Watchdog validation per mission.
- For finance and governance-heavy teams, maintain assumption traceability, source citations, and redline logs.
