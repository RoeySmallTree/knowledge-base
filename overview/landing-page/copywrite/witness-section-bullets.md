### Voice Guidelines

- **State plainly.** Let the architecture speak
- **Technical matter-of-fact.** Describe structure, not benefits
- **No declarations.** Avoid phrases that announce or proclaim
- **No comparisons.** Don't frame against anything

## ALTERNATIVE STRUCTURAL APPROACHES

### Approach 1: Capability Threshold Framing
Each card declares what becomes possible at this architectural generation:

1. **Superposition** — Parallel multi-model execution
2. **Continuity** — Unbounded context and memory
3. **Structure** — Project-organized intelligence
4. **Emergence** — Brain-mimicking multi-disciplinary dynamics
5. **Legibility** — Native transparency and integration

*Pros:* Pure capability statement, no comparison required  
*Cons:* May feel abstract without technical grounding

### Approach 2: Architectures of Mind
Cards describe architectural principles drawn from cognition:

1. **Multiplicity** — Many processes, unified direction
2. **Continuity** — Memory that persists and scales
3. **Crystallization** — Thought that structures itself
4. **Coordination** — Specialized minds in concert
5. **Legibility** — Reasoning made visible

*Pros:* Strong brain-mimicking throughline  
*Cons:* Requires reader to make the technical connection

### Approach 3: The New Primitives
Lead with what the architecture enables as building blocks:

1. **Parallel Processing** — Multi-model concurrent execution
2. **Infinite Context** — Scaling without boundaries
3. **Structured Output** — Projects replace conversations
4. **Emergent Behavior** — Cognition-like dynamics
5. **Native Transparency** — Legibility by design

*Pros:* Technical clarity, modular understanding  
*Cons:* Reads more like a spec than a narrative
