# The Newsroom

> *"Choose your editorial lens: dry finance or sensational drama."*

## Overview

The Newsroom extends the Daily Dispatch with **configurable editorial stance**. The same facts can be presented through different editorial lenses—Bloomberg-style dry financial analysis, TechCrunch startup hype, or tabloid sensationalism.

## Tags
`#cortex` `#news` `#research` `#editorial` `#content`

## Use Cases
- Understanding how different media would cover a story
- Content creation with specific tone
- Media training and preparation
- Competitive narrative analysis
- Communication strategy development

## Editorial Modes

| Mode | Style | Use Case |
|------|-------|----------|
| **Bloomberg** | Dry, data-focused, institutional | Financial analysis |
| **TechCrunch** | Startup hype, founder-friendly | Tech industry |
| **Tabloid** | Dramatic, emotional, personality-focused | Consumer understanding |
| **Academic** | Careful, caveated, peer-reviewed style | Research credibility |

## Composition

| Role | Agent | Function |
|------|-------|----------|
| Chair | Editor-in-Chief | Sets editorial stance and tone for the publication |
| Operative | Beat Reporter | Reports facts (consistent across modes) |
| Operative | Pundit | Analysis styled to match editorial mode |
| Operative | Archivist | Context and background |
| Watchdog | Fact-Checker | Accuracy regardless of editorial mode |

## Expected Outputs
1. **Front Page** — Top stories in selected editorial style
2. **Deep Dives** — Extended coverage with mode-appropriate tone
3. **Morgue** — Source archive
4. **Mode Comparison** — Same story in multiple editorial styles

## Sample Prompts
- "Cover our product launch in Bloomberg style"
- "How would TechCrunch write about this funding round?"
- "Show me this story from tabloid vs. academic perspectives"
- "Prepare me for how different outlets might cover this"
