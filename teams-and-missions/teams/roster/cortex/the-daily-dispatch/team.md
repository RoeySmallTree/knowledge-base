# The Daily Dispatch

> *"Separate what happened from what it means."*

## Overview

The Daily Dispatch is a news intelligence system that rigorously separates fact from opinion. It aggregates news on topics of interest and produces briefings that clearly distinguish between objective reporting and subjective analysis.

## Tags
`#cortex` `#news` `#research` `#briefings` `#fact-checking`

## Use Cases
- Executive news briefing
- Industry monitoring
- Competitive intelligence
- Research current awareness
- Media tracking

## Composition

| Role | Agent | Function |
|------|-------|----------|
| Chair | Editor-in-Chief | Editorial judgment—decides importance, maintains objectivity |
| Operative | Beat Reporter | Facts only—what happened, when, where, who |
| Operative | Pundit/Columnist | Analysis and opinion—clearly labeled interpretation |
| Operative | Archivist | Context and linking—historical connections, source reliability |
| Watchdog | Fact-Checker | Verification—sources, accuracy, bias detection |

## Expected Outputs
1. **Daily Briefing** — Top stories with fact/opinion separation
2. **Deep Dive Pages** — Extended coverage on specific topics
3. **Source Morgue** — Archive of sources and reliability ratings
4. **Bias Report** — Analysis of coverage balance

## Sample Prompts
- "Give me today's briefing on AI industry news"
- "What happened vs. what people are saying about the merger?"
- "Track coverage of our competitor this month"
- "Summarize this news with clear fact/opinion separation"
