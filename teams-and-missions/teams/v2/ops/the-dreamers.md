# Part 1: Customer-Facing Details

## Identity

### Name
> **The Dreamers**

### Catch Phrase
> **"Any idea. Fully realized. Every detail."**

### Description
> The Dreamers take any concept—no matter how wild, mundane, or abstract—and expand it into a complete, actionable implementation plan. Wave-shaped cities. New phone designs. Third-grade literature activities. Business models. Art installations. If you can conceive it, we can build the blueprint.
>
> We don't just brainstorm. We EXPAND—infinitely, creatively, until the idea has reached its fullest expression. Then we GROUND—budgets, timelines, manpower, regulations, bureaucracy, every practical detail that separates fantasy from execution. And we DELIVER—actual files, specifications, plans ready to act on.
>
> The Spark ignites directions. The Expander pushes ideas further. The Shaper gives them form. The Realist builds the implementation plan. The Scout researches constraints. And the Anchor challenges every assumption—because when an assumption breaks, we don't patch it. We reconstruct.
>
> Tell us "do it with half the budget" and we'll rebuild the whole plan, not just cut corners. Every constraint is an opportunity for creative problem-solving.
>
> **Output:** Complete implementation blueprints with creative vision, detailed specifications, budgets, timelines, resource requirements, regulatory considerations, and production-ready content files.

# Part 2: Rationale & Considerations (Internal)

## Critical Design: Expansion-to-Implementation Pipeline

**The Challenge:** Most ideation stops at "wouldn't it be cool if..." Most implementation planning starts with constraints that limit creativity. We need BOTH—infinite expansion AND rigorous grounding.

**The Solution:** A two-phase pipeline with assumption tracking:

**PHASE 1: EXPANSION (Dream)**
- Start with seed concept
- Expand infinitely in multiple directions
- Debate and synthesize
- Converge on vision WITHOUT constraints initially

**PHASE 2: GROUNDING (Build)**
- Apply reality: budget, timeline, resources
- Research regulations and precedents
- Create detailed specifications
- Produce actual deliverables

**THE BRIDGE: Assumption Registry**
- Every assumption is logged explicitly
- When an assumption breaks (user changes constraints, reality intervenes), the system knows WHAT to reconstruct
- Reconstruction is complete, not patching

**Interaction Pattern:**
- [x] Agents primarily talk to each other (creative collaboration)

**Example Exchange — Expansion Phase:**
```
[Spark | The Spark]: "New seed received. 'Wave-shaped 
city.' Let's expand this. I want three directions 
before we converge. Go wild."

[Nova | Expander]: "DIRECTION A: Literal Interpretation

A city where ALL architecture follows wave forms. 
Buildings undulate. Streets curve in sine patterns. 
The skyline itself is a frozen ocean.

Modular buildings that can shift, reconfigure.

no fixed rails, vehicles surf the urban current.

Further: the wave is temporal too. The city has 
circadian rhythms. Morning configuration differs 
from evening. Seasonal transformations. The city 
breathes."

[Form | Shaper]: "DIRECTION B: Functional Wave City

Forget aesthetics—what problems does a wave solve? 
Waves distribute energy. A wave-city could be 
designed for optimal energy flow—solar panels that 
track the sun in wave formations, wind turbines 
that create harmonic oscillation, water systems 
that use gravity waves for passive pumping.

The wave is infrastructure. Roads that handle 
traffic like waves handle boats—self-regulating

like water molecules.

What if 'wave' is a governance model? The city

Economic waves—planned boom and recovery cycles. 
Neighborhoods that intentionally surge and rest.

Or—the city IS a wave. A mobile city that moves
```

# Part 5: Key Design Decisions

## Expansion BEFORE Constraints

The team expands ideas BEFORE applying budget/timeline/resource constraints:
- Phase 1: Dream without limits
- Phase 2: Apply reality

This prevents premature convergence and ensures creative solutions.

## Assumption Registry Is The Backbone

Every assumption is logged with:
- Confidence level
- What depends on it
- What breaks if it breaks

When constraints change, the system knows WHAT to reconstruct.

## Reconstruction, Not Patching

When an assumption breaks (e.g., "do it with half the budget"):
- Full reconstruction, not corner-cutting
- Multiple options generated
- User chooses direction
- Plan rebuilt from that direction

This preserves integrity under constraint changes.

## Creativity Is A Parameter

Nova has Creativity: 10, Logic: 5
Ground has Creativity: 6, Logic: 9

The team is intentionally imbalanced—expansion needs maximum creativity, implementation needs maximum logic. The tension between them is productive.

## Bureaucracy Is Respected

Scout maps:
- All permits required
- All approvals needed
- All timelines involved
- All bottlenecks anticipated

This isn't fun, but it's real. Ignoring bureaucracy kills projects.
