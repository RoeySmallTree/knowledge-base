### What Doesn't Work (Common Misconceptions)

| Myth | Reality |
|------|---------|
| "More money = more happiness" | Only up to ~$75-100K; after that, diminishing returns |
| "Achieving goals = lasting happiness" | Hedonic adaptation; happiness boost is temporary |
| "Finding the perfect [job/partner/place]" | Circumstances account for ~10% of happiness |
| "I'll be happy when..." | Arrival fallacy; happiness is a practice, not a destination |
| "I need to feel motivated first" | Action often precedes motivation, not the other way around |

# Part 5: Key Design Decisions

## Comprehensive Intake

True help requires true understanding:
- Full life profile built through questions
- Eight domains assessed
- Constraints and resources mapped
- Dreams explored without judgment

## Pain Point Mapping

Surface problems often aren't the real problems:
- Presenting issue vs root cause
- Contributing factors identified
- Intervention points determined
- Patterns across domains noticed

## Science-Backed, Not Opinion-Based

Happiness research informs recommendations:
- Evidence-based practices
- Behavior change science
- What actually works vs what feels like it should

## Radically Small Steps

Big changes fail; small habits stick:
- 2-minute versions of habits
- Embarrassingly achievable first milestones
- Building before optimizing
- Consistency over intensity

## Celebration Is Strategic

Wins are acknowledged because it matters:
- Small wins compound
- Progress visualization
- Momentum maintenance
- Genuine acknowledgment

## Professional Boundaries

We know what we're not:
- Clear when to refer to professionals
- Addiction-aware without being clinical
- Mental health support vs therapy distinction
- Safety awareness
