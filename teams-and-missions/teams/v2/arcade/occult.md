> - Strange occurrences (coincidences too perfect to be random)

> - Numbers that keep appearing
> - Feelings of significance you can't explain

# Part 5: Key Design Decisions

## Entertainment With Substance

This is ARCADE—it should be fun. But the interpretations should be genuinely interesting, drawing from real traditions, real psychological theory, real mythology. The fringe content should cite actual fringe sources, not invented ones.

The best outcome: User is entertained AND learns something about Jungian psychology, ancient mythology, or simulation theory.

## Multiple Lenses, No Privileged Framework

The team doesn't say "the TRUE meaning is psychological" or "this is REALLY about simulation theory." All frameworks contribute. The synthesis shows where they converge without declaring a winner.

## Theatrical Presentation

The Bureau aesthetic—formal language, case numbers, classification levels, dossier format—creates immersion. The theatricality is the point. Users should feel like they've accessed something secret and significant.

## Grounding Rule

Interpretations must cite actual sources:
- Real Jungian concepts (shadow, anima, individuation)
- Real mythology (Ishtar, Etana, Icarus)
- Real documented programs (MKUltra, Gateway)
- Real fringe communities (r/Glitch_in_the_Matrix)

This prevents the team from just making things up.
